    • Story of Us

    It was Thursday afternoon when you came to me asking me to join you on some mysterious errand. Your mysterious plans were nothing new to me.


    What could be this important on an average Thursday afternoon? This is what I thought to myself.


    Instead of searching for answers, I decided to focus on my previous plans.


    “I would love to, really, I would but I am busy with job applications.”


    More than anything, my response was due to the fact I needed to focus on securing my future, since I was only a few months out from graduating. I had no room for frivolous adventures. However, you convinced me that the errand wouldn’t take long and that my job applications could wait. All you wanted was some company.


    While looking into your emerald eyes, there was sincerity mixed with a bit of mischief, however I decided to rebel against my better judgement.


    “Okay, you’ve got me for one hour max.”

    You smiled as we hopped into your car. I turned to you as we began driving through the city which bled and passed over the barrier into the countryside. We kept going farther from our hometown.


    “So, what are we doing exactly?” I asked.

    “We’re going to watch the sunset and observe the stars!” he responded. His eyes twinkled with excitement.


    I should’ve known.

    I didn’t even bother responding. I just shook my head and watched the changing of the skies, as the previous blue began to fade.


    After 15 minutes, we arrived at an open field. The lush grass spanned for miles wet from early morning rain. The sky began to fade into a pastel purple as the clouds painted the sky.


    As we sat side by side, nothing was said. We simply watched the sky fade until it reached its truest form of darkness. We both sat there in awe.


    Within the darkness, stars began to glow and glitter in the sky. And the silence was the epitome of peace, as fireflies surrounded our presence dancing in the night.


    ~Jess
